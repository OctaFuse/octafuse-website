## Product overview

**OctaFuse** is an **AI gateway** for teams and enterprises: a single place to expose model traffic to your products while centralizing how you **authenticate, budget, route, meter, and observe** that traffic. Teams keep familiar chat and completion calling patterns; operators get consistent controls and reporting instead of duplicating glue in every service.

It fits organizations juggling **multiple providers, models, and business lines** but wanting **one governance and operations story**.

## Why OctaFuse

- **Less duplicated work**: stop re-implementing vendor wiring, keys, and limits in every app.
- **Meet clients where they are**: support common OpenAI-, Anthropic-, and Gemini-shaped calling styles so migrations and coexistence are smoother.
- **Clearer cost and risk**: budgets, usage, and investigations share a single lens; tiered cost views support capacity planning and reconciliation conversations.
- **More operable resilience**: central routing priorities and safer failover paths when upstreams change or wobble—without every team patching ad hoc.

## Deployment options

Choose based on **compliance, latency, data residency, and how you want to operate infrastructure**. Step-by-step commands live in this site’s docs.

- **Docker on a laptop or private network**: fastest way to try OctaFuse, run PoCs, and debug on a shared internal environment.
- **Managed Cloudflare**: great when you want an edge-friendly footprint and a managed database backing configuration and metadata.
- **Self-hosted on your stack**: best when you need stricter data boundaries, VPC-style networking, or alignment with existing database operations (commonly Postgres or MySQL).

Read [Deployment](/en/docs/deploy/) for trade-offs and [Quick start](/en/docs/quick-start/) for the shortest path to a working setup. Licensed under **GNU AGPL v3** — see [LICENSE](https://github.com/OctaFuse/octafuse-gateway/blob/main/LICENSE).

## Who it’s for

- Platform and product orgs that need **one governed front door** for model traffic across many lines of business or tenants.
- Teams that want **cost, reliability, and auditability** lifted out of application code into dedicated infrastructure.
- Organizations that want **freedom to evolve vendors and models** without bespoke failover logic in every service.
